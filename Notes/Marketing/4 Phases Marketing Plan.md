A simple-to-understand marketing plan with 4 phases that'll help you start smarter, and pre-promote your book/novel before you even finish writing it.
# Phase 1

## Foundation and Positioning
Goal: Build the base before telling people about the book.

*This phase should happen **2 to 3 months before release**.*

### Define the Book Clearly

You need one clear sentence that sells the story.

Example: **Twenty-five years after the world collapsed, two strangers must cross a ruined city filled with gangs, flooded streets, and creatures that hunt by song.**

This line will be used everywhere.

Website  
social media  
Amazon page

---

## Build a Simple Author Website

Create a clean page with:
- book cover    
- short description    
- email signup    
- first chapter download    

Title of the page: **Enter the World of 25 Years After**

Readers who like dark survival stories will want to see the setting.

---

## Create Visual Identity

Use the same tone everywhere.

Main themes
- ruined cities    
- dark sky    
- flooded streets    
- two survivors walking    

Colors
- black    
- dark blue    
- rust red    

This helps readers recognize your book instantly.

---

## Prepare Launch Material

Before the promotion begins, prepare:

- final book cover    
- Amazon description    
- author bio    
- social media images    
- 5 short quotes from the book    

These will be used later in promotion.

---

# Phase 2

## Audience Building

Goal: Start attracting readers **before the book releases**.

Start this phase **2 months before launch**.

---

## Social Media Strategy

Focus on platforms where visual storytelling works well:

- TikTok    
- Instagram    
- YouTube Shorts    

Post **3 to 4 times per week**.

### Video Idea 1

Story hook example:

“Twenty-five years after the world ended, two strangers meet during a storm in an abandoned house.”

Then ask: **Would you trust a stranger in the apocalypse?**

---

### Video Idea 2

World Building: Explain parts of the setting.

Examples:
- the ruined city of [[Ashenfall]]    
- the flooded districts    
- the survival gangs   

These short clips build curiosity.

---

### Video Idea 3

- Atmosphere Clips
- Use dark visuals of ruined cities.
- Add text from the book.

Example:

“Somewhere in the storm two strangers found shelter. In this world that could mean hope. Or death.”

---

## Email List

Start collecting readers.

Offer:

**Free Chapter Download**

Email sequence example:

- Email 1: Welcome to the wasteland.
- Email 2: Who Raven is.
- Email 3: Who Jason is.
- Email 4: The city of [[Ashenfall]].
- Email 5: Book launch announcement.

---

## Community Presence

Join reader spaces where survival fiction fans gather.

Good places
- Reddit dystopian fiction groups.
- Goodreads apocalypse groups.
- Facebook survival fiction communities.

Post interesting content

Examples:
- maps of the ruined city    
- survival questions    
- character art   
Do not promote constantly. Just be active.

---

# Phase 3

## Launch Campaign

Goal: Get strong visibility in the first week.

Launch week affects how Amazon recommends the book.

---

## ARC Readers

Give early copies to about **40 to 60 readers**.
They read before launch and leave reviews.

Places to find them
- Goodreads reviewers    
- Reddit book communities    
- indie reader groups   

Ask them to post reviews during launch week.

---

## Launch Price

Use a lower price during the first week.

Example:

- 5.99 or 9.99

Lower price increases early sales and ranking.
After the first week, raise the price.

---

## Amazon Categories

Choose smaller categories where ranking is easier.

Examples:
- post apocalyptic fiction    
- dystopian survival fiction    
- dark adventure fiction    

Goal: Reach top ten in these categories.

---

## Launch Week Content

During launch week post daily content.

Examples:

- Day 1: Book launch announcement.
- Day 2: Quote from the novel.
- Day 3: Introduce [[Raven]].
- Day 4: Introduce [[Jason]].
- Day 5: Describe [[Ashenfall]].
- Day 6: Reader reactions.
- Day 7: Thank readers and share reviews.

---

# Phase 4

# Long Term Growth

Goal: Keep the book alive after launch.
Many books fail because promotion stops too early.

---

## Content Around the World

Create posts about the universe of the story.

Examples:
- the gangs of [[Ashenfall]].
- the flooded districts.
- survival rules in this world.
- what caused the collapse.

Readers love lore.

---

## Blog Articles

Write articles connected to the theme of the book.

Examples:
- why readers love post apocalyptic stories.
- how ruined cities inspire fiction.
- survival psychology in dark worlds.

At the end of each article link to the book.

---

## Expand the World

If readers enjoy the story, you can release:

- short stories in the same world.
- character backstories.
- maps of ruined cities.

This keeps readers interested while you write the next book.

---

## Build Toward a Series

Your story already feels like a series.

Readers who enjoy survival fiction usually want long journeys.

Future books could explore
- new ruined cities.
- new survivor groups.
- the deeper mystery behind the world's collapse.

A series builds a loyal audience.

---

# Summary

The strategy is simple.

Phase 1  
Build the foundation.

Phase 2  
Attract readers and build curiosity.

Phase 3  
Launch with reviews and visibility.

Phase 4  
Keep readers engaged and grow the world.
