### Main Backstory
Raven was only six years old when the world collapsed in 2000. She lived in a small farming community outside Sacramento with her parents, Maria and Carlos, and her younger brother Miguel. Her father worked as a mechanic, her mother as a schoolteacher. They had a garden, chickens, and dreams of Miguel starting kindergarten that autumn.

When the resource wars began and the experimental weapons were unleashed, their community tried to remain self-sufficient. For three years, they managed. Raven learned to tend the garden, to preserve food, to read by candlelight when the power grids failed permanently. Her parents shielded her from the worst of it, maintaining routines and hope even as refugees poured through their town with stories of cities burning.

At nine, Raven watched government soldiers execute her father for "hoarding" the community's food stores. Her mother gathered what they could carry, took her children, and fled into the wilderness. They survived two winters in a makeshift camp with other families, but dysentery claimed Miguel when he was only seven. Maria never recovered from the loss, growing thinner and more distant until she simply walked into the forest one morning and never returned.

Raven, now eleven and alone, was taken in by Elena Vasquez, a former nurse who had established a small sanctuary for orphaned children in an abandoned school. Elena taught her that kindness was not weakness, that helping others was how humans stayed human. For four years, Raven learned medicine, self-defense, and the delicate art of maintaining hope in hopeless times.

When Raven was fifteen, raiders found their sanctuary. Elena died defending the children, but not before teaching Raven the most important lesson: "You survive not just for yourself, but for everyone who didn't make it this far." Raven escaped with three younger children, leading them across two states to safety before they were separated by a militia group that conscripted anyone over fourteen.

She spent three years with the Colorado Defense Coalition, learning combat medicine and witnessing humanity at both its worst and finest. She saved lives during the Denver Siege, pulling wounded from burning buildings while mortars fell around them. She also watched good people make terrible choices, saw children forced to become soldiers, and learned that survival sometimes required doing things that would haunt her dreams.

At eighteen, she deserted during a mission that would have required executing civilian families suspected of collaboration. She's spent the last thirteen years moving through the wasteland, sometimes alone, sometimes with small groups, always carrying Elena's medical bag and the belief that every life saved is a victory against the darkness.
