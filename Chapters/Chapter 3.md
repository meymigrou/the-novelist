## City of the Drowned

The highway began to slope downward as they moved deeper into [[Ashenfall]], bringing them closer to the flooded zone where the real dangers waited. Here, the buildings stood waist-deep in black water that reflected nothing, as if it had absorbed all the light that had ever touched it and refused to give any back.

From their elevated position, they could see the Tide Runners' territory spread out below them like a water-logged labyrinth. Boats moved between the buildings, some clearly scavenged from the marina that had once served weekend fishermen, others cobbled together from materials never meant to float. The people in them moved with the fluid grace of those who had learned to call the water home, even water that could kill you if you swallowed too much.

But it was the sounds rising from the flooded district that made Raven's blood run cold. Laughter that echoed off the submerged buildings, but laughter with too many teeth in it. Splashing that sounded deliberate, predatory. And underneath it all, a rhythmic pounding that might have been drums or might have been something far worse.

"They're hunting," Jason said, watching a pack of small boats converge on something moving through the water near what had once been the city's main library. The building's upper floors still rose above the flood, its windows dark except for the occasional flicker of light that suggested someone or something still lived inside.

The something in the water was human, Raven realized, watching through the binoculars as a figure tried desperately to swim toward the library's entrance. But the Tide Runners were faster, their boats cutting through the water like sharks circling wounded prey.

"We have to help," she said, but even as the words left her mouth, she knew it was already too late.

The boats closed in, and the screaming started. It echoed off the water and the buildings, multiplied and amplified until it seemed to come from everywhere at once. Then, abruptly, it stopped.

The boats dispersed, leaving behind only ripples on the black water and the knowledge that they had just witnessed the kind of casual brutality that had become commonplace in this drowned world.

=="We can't save everyone," Jason said softly, but Raven heard the pain in his voice. It was the same pain she carried, the weight of all the people they couldn't help, all the horrors they had to witness in silence.==

They continued along the highway, which began to show more serious damage as they progressed. Chunks of roadway had fallen into the water below, leaving gaps they had to jump or climb around. Some sections were so damaged they had to crawl across on hands and knees, trying not to look through the holes in the concrete at the poisoned water far below.

It was during one of these careful traverses that they first heard the singing.

It drifted up from the flooded streets, beautiful and haunting, the kind of melody that made you think of churches and childhood and everything good the world had once held. But there was something wrong with it, something that made the hair on the back of Raven's neck stand up.

"Sirens," Jason whispered, recognizing the trap for what it was.

Below them, figures moved through the water with impossible grace, their pale forms just visible beneath the surface. They sang as they swam, their voices harmonizing in ways that human voices shouldn't be able to achieve. And scattered around the buildings near them, bones gleamed white in the dark water, picked clean and polished by years of tides.

The Tide Runners weren't just humans who had adapted to life on the water, Raven realized. They were something else entirely, something that had learned to use beauty as bait and song as a weapon.

One of the singers surfaced near a half-submerged car, and in the brief moment before it disappeared again, Raven saw its face: human features stretched and distorted, gills fluttering along its neck, and eyes that had gone completely black, adapted for seeing in the perpetual twilight of the flooded city.

"Don't listen," Jason said, pressing his hands over his ears. "Whatever you do, don't listen to the song."

But it was hard to ignore something so beautiful, so perfectly crafted to appeal to every lonely, desperate part of the human heart. The melody spoke of rest, of peace, of an end to all the running and hiding and struggling just to see another sunrise.

Raven found herself slowing, her feet moving toward the edge of the highway, toward the water where the beautiful voices waited. It would be so easy to let go, to slip into that black embrace and let the singers take away all the pain and fear and responsibility of staying alive in a world that seemed determined to kill everything good.

Jason's hand on her arm brought her back to herself with a jolt. He had one hand clamped over his own ear and was using the other to pull her away from the edge, his face twisted with the effort of resisting the song's pull.

Together, they ran through the ruins of Ashenfall's [[Financial District]], the singers' voices following them like a tide of poisoned honey. Behind them, the water began to churn as pale forms broke the surface, no longer content to wait for prey to come to them.

The highway curved ahead, leading them toward the eastern districts and away from the flooded zone. But between them and safety lay the most dangerous part of their journey: the Bone Yards, where the Rust Eaters had built their empire on the corpses of the old world.

As they ran, Raven could hear the Tide Runners emerging from the water, their webbed feet slapping against wet concrete as they gave chase. Their song had changed now, no longer beautiful but hungry, a predator's call that promised only death.

The highway began to rise again, carrying them up and away from the flooded district, but the damage here was worse. Entire sections had collapsed, forcing them to climb through twisted rebar and broken concrete while their pursuers gained ground below.

Just when it seemed the singers would catch them, a new sound cut through their melody: the roar of engines and the rattle of automatic gunfire. The Rust Eaters had noticed the commotion and were moving to defend their territory, not out of any desire to help the travelers but because the Tide Runners were trespassing on their hunting grounds.

Raven and Jason used the distraction to put more distance between themselves and their pursuers, climbing higher into the maze of mid-level buildings where new dangers waited. Behind them, the battle between the water dwellers and the scavenger gangs lit up the flooded district with muzzle flashes and the screams of things that had once been human.

They had survived the Tide Runners, but Ashenfall was far from done with them. Ahead, in the concrete canyons between the leaning towers, the Rust Eaters were already setting their traps.
